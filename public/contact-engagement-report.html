About Engagement Score

The engagement score is a proprietary metric calculated based on recipient interaction with emails. Scores range from 0-70 with the following breakdown:

  • 0 points: No engagement (did not open email)
  • 10 points: Opened email but did not click
  • 20-30 points: Opened and clicked one link
  • 40-50 points: Opened and clicked multiple links
  • 60-70 points: Deep engagement (multiple opens and clicks)

Higher scores indicate deeper engagement with email content.

Contact Engagement Recommendations

  1. Leverage high engagers for event marketing: The data shows event-related emails generate the highest engagement. Target the top 20 most engaged contacts with exclusive event invitations.
  2. Re-engage inactive contacts: Create a targeted campaign for the 94 non-engaging contacts with compelling subject lines and personalized content.
  3. Optimize for mobile: With 58.3% of contacts reading emails on mobile devices, ensure all campaigns are mobile-optimized.
  4. Follow-up strategy: The success of the RSVP follow-up (32.9% click rate) demonstrates the value of well-timed follow-ups. Implement this approach for other important campaigns.
  5. Client segmentation: Create engagement-based segments (High, Medium, Low) in your email marketing platform to deliver more targeted content.
  6. Frequency optimization: The most engaged contacts received 8-14 emails in the 30-day period. Consider increasing frequency for high engagers while reducing it for low engagers.
