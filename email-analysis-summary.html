About Engagement Score

The engagement score is a proprietary metric calculated based on recipient interaction with emails. Scores range from 0-50 with the following breakdown:

  • 0 points: No engagement (did not open email)
  • 10 points: Opened email but did not click
  • 30-50 points: Opened and clicked links (varies based on click count and time spent)

Higher scores indicate deeper engagement with email content. The average across all campaigns is calculated as a weighted average based on recipient count.

Key Insights

Event RSVP campaign was most effective: The "RSVP: June 18 Double Header With HMA!" campaign had the highest click rate (33.0%) and engagement score (16.5), indicating strong interest in the Wrigley Field event. This suggests clients are highly responsive to event invitations.

Follow-up RSVP campaign was highly effective: The "Re: RSVP" follow-up campaign achieved an impressive 32.9% click rate, nearly matching the original campaign. This demonstrates the effectiveness of timely follow-up messages for event-based communications.

April Newsletter performed well: The April Newsletter reached a large audience with good open rates (56%) and an impressive 12.8% click rate, significantly above industry average. This suggests the content and links were compelling to recipients.

Event invitations drive high engagement: The RSVP campaign showed excellent open rates (65.0%) and exceptional click rates (33.0%). This confirms that clients are highly interested in in-person events, and the June 18 Wrigley Field event is particularly appealing.

Click rates are outstanding: Overall, email campaigns are driving excellent open rates (56.5%, well above industry average) with impressive click rates (8.9%, more than 3x the industry average). This shows strong engagement with HMA's email content.

Cross-campaign engagement is low: Only 66 recipients (5.7% of total audience) engaged with multiple campaigns, suggesting opportunity to increase cross-campaign engagement and content relevance.

Recommendations

  1. Continue event-based marketing: Build on the success of the Wrigley Field event campaign with more event-focused initiatives, leveraging the 33% click rate.
  2. Learn from April Newsletter success: Apply techniques from the successful April Newsletter to future newsletters. Identify what specific content drove the 12.8% click rate.
  3. Continue follow-up RSVP strategy: The Re: RSVP follow-up email was extremely successful with a 32.9% click rate. Continue this approach for future events.
  4. Increase cross-campaign engagement: Create more cohesive email journeys that encourage engagement across multiple campaigns to boost the 5.7% of recipients who engage with multiple emails.
  5. Test different send times: Experiment with different send times and days to optimize open rates, particularly for campaigns with open rates below 60%.
  6. A/B test subject lines: Test different subject line approaches to improve open rates for campaigns below 60%, particularly the Well-th Vault campaign (44.7%).
